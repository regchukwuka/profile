Reginald Chukwuka Molokwu

Reginald is a dedicated researcher who excels in gleaning useful information from large amounts of data. Because data has grown so much, and annotating it is time-consuming and costly, he's interested in machine learning and how computers may learn to learn by themselves with less data (Meta-learning). Here, Reginald has tried several computational experiments and approaches to try to address the issue at hand.

Reginald has also mostly worked in small and medium-sized companies as a data scientist/Machine learning engineer. Throughout his career, he has shown a strong ability to create statistical models and do descriptive data analysis in order to meet organizational objectives. Reginald had his Bachelor of Science with Honours in Computer Science (CGPA = 3.7/5) from the prestigious Chukwuemeka Odumegwu Ojukwu University, Uli (formerly Anambra State University, Uli), Nigeria

For the last 5 years, he has been a staunch believer in artificial intelligence (AI) as the new electricity that will power all sectors, and he is keen to help corporations and research organizations win this race. However, we can't win this race unless we train computers how to make the most of the little data they have.

Reginald is the author of two research papers that have appeared in prestigious Key Journals in his field.

In a nutshell, I like identifying patterns in data. This data ranges from human character traits (Sociology) to computer behaviors (Computer science). Life, in my opinion, is replete with patterns, and any behavior shown by an entity/node can be traced back to its source. Regardless of how intelligent you are, things do not happen by luck!

My attitude is that nothing in life is impossible; the difficulty is in comprehending the job at hand and devising incremental methods to do it. This may be accomplished by dividing a work down into smaller portions, using algorithms, and keeping track of important details.
