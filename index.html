Reginald Chukwuka Molokwu

Reginald is a devoted researcher who excels at extracting information from large datasets. Because annotating data is time consuming and expensive, he is interested in machine learning and how computers may learn to learn without as much data (Meta-learning). Reginald has tried several computational experiments to address the issue.

His experience is in small and medium businesses as a data scientist/machine learning engineer. Throughout his career, he has shown a remarkable capacity to develop statistical models and analyze descriptive data.

Reginald earned a 3.86/5 CGPA Bachelor of Science Honours in Computer Science from Chukwuemeka Odumegwu Ojukwu University, Uli (formerly Anambra State University, Uli).

He has been a strong believer in artificial intelligence (AI) for the past 5 years, as the new electricity that will power all sectors of the economy, and he wants to assist companies and research groups win this race. But we can't win this race unless we teach computers to learn with limited data.

Reginald has published two research papers in prestigious Key Journals in his field.

Reginald in a nutshell, enjoys finding patterns in data. This data varies from human (Sociology) to computer behavior (Computer science). He thinks life is full of patterns, and that each action from an entity/node has a causative factor. No matter how smart you are, things don't just happen!

Reginald's belief is that nothing is impossible; the challenge is understanding the task at hand and creating progressive solutions. This may be achieved by segmenting tasks, employing algorithms, and keeping track of crucial information.
